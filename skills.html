During this semester there were many significant aspects of website development we learned. Initially, we went over historical aspects of the internet. While covering this topic, we discussed how early technologies set the stage for the technology we use today. People like Grace Hopper, who created the language necessary for us to type and buid programs with was also a topic of discussion at this stage. After learning about this, we got a better understanding of what our technology derived from and where it has arrived.

GIT, GITHUB, AFS & LOCAL AND REMOTE HOSTS

After learning the historical context of the internet, we began to utilize tools pertaining to internet development. The first one we observed was Git (Webstorm). This integrated development environment (IDE) helped us hone our developing skills in an array of different ways. One way was in our ability to manage projects as a team. We established our groups and began to work on one of numerous group assignments, making commits and pushing and pulling data to reflect our contributions. The projects got gradually more complex as the semester went on. We were introduced to new commands and html coding pretty much every week. Next, we learned how t utilize our Andrew file system (AFS) profiles to host web pages from our school's server. This was an important lesson because we learned how to move files seamlessly across different hosts without having to drag and drop files. It also taught us how to create files and directories without having to constantly click, and also how to track these files and directories. A number of weeks passed until we had a good understanding of how to perform these tasks, and then we moved on to cascading style sheets (CSS) coding. This stage was when we learned how to right-click and inspect pages to make more streamlined customizations to our web pages. We then dabbled in different page layouts and observed new ways to facilitate our coding such as bootstrap. Towards the end of the semester we began working on our final individual and group projects, which this actual page in particular represents!
